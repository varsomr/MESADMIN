Emergency Situations

An incident affecting a business critical application or service that impacts a high number of Users for which a delay in restoring service is not acceptable. It needs to be resolved as soon as possible. It has a significant relationship to the business which could lead to negative production, brand or financial impact. Typically a serious outage or major loss of functionality in a business critical application that has no workaround and must be restored for work to continue.
An incident affecting a business important application or a service is significantly degraded wherein a high number of Users cannot carry out normal work responsibilities, there is no workaround or alternative available and for which a delay in services being restored is not acceptable.

Non Emergency

Priority 3 Medium: An incident affecting normal (non-critical) applications and a limited number of Users, systems or components are down or degraded performance, but the requestor can carry out some work responsibilities and/or a temporary workaround is available. Usually an impact to a single User that does not involve business critical applications. Priority 4 Low: An incident with Low or no visibility and no direct impact on systems, Customers, Users or revenue. Example is a end-user documentation guide has some misleading/erroneous details that need to be updated.
